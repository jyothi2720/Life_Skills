

# Questions:
1. What kinds of behaviour cause sexual harassment?
2. What would you do in case you face or witness any incident or repeated incidents of such behaviour?


## 1. What kinds of behaviour cause sexual harassment?

* Unwanted physical contact such as touching, hugging, kissing, pinching, or coming too close to someone.

* Sexual comments or jokes about a person’s body, clothes, or personal life that make them uncomfortable.

* Asking or pressuring for sexual favours, directly or indirectly.

* Staring, leering, or making sexual gestures that create discomfort.

* Sharing or showing sexual content like images, videos, or messages at the workplace or online.

* Repeated unwanted attention such as following someone, watching them, or giving inappropriate gifts with sexual intent.

## 2. What would you do in case you face or witness any incident or repeated incidents of such behaviour?

* **Speak up if safe –** Clearly tell the person to stop and move away to a safe place.

* **Ensure safety and support –** Inform a trusted colleague or help the affected person if you are a witness.

* **Record the incident –** Note down what happened, when and where it happened, and who was involved.

* **Keep evidence –** Save messages, emails, photos, or any proof related to the incident.

* **Report officially –** Inform the Internal Complaints Committee (ICC), HR, or supervisor as per the POSH policy.

* **Seek help and don’t stay silent –** Reach out to trusted people or professionals; reporting helps prevent repeat incidents.

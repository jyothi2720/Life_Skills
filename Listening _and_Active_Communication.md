## 1. What are the steps/strategies to do Active Listening?

* Pay full attention and focus only on the person talking.

* Look at the person to show you are listening.

* Do not interrupt; let the person finish talking.

* Show you are listening by nodding or saying “okay” or “I see.”

* Ask questions if you do not understand something.

* Repeat or say the main idea in your own words.

* Watch the person’s body language and tone to understand feelings.

* Give polite feedback and answer nicely.

## 2. According to Fisher's model, what are the key points of Reflective Listening?
* Listen carefully to what the person is saying without judging.

* Notice the person’s feelings and emotions behind their words.

* Repeat or say in your own words what you understood.

* Show understanding and make the person feel heard.

* Give the person time and space to talk and explain more.

## 3. What are the obstacles in your listening process?

* I get distracted by my phone or things around me.

* I think about what to say next instead of listening.

* I guess things before the person finishes talking.

* Sometimes I am in a hurry and do not listen properly.

* Long talks make me lose focus.

## 4. What can you do to improve your listening?

* Keep my phone away and avoid distractions.

* Take notes to stay focused.

* Wait before answering and do not rush.

* Ask small questions to stay involved in the conversation.

* Look at the person and say small words like “okay” or “hmm.”

* Repeat what the person said to make sure I understood correctly.

## 5. When do you switch to Passive communication style in your day to day life?

* I stay quiet when I want to avoid arguments or fights.

* I stay silent when I feel shy or unsure about my opinion.

* I do not speak to avoid hurting someone’s feelings.

* I stay quiet around strict or controlling people.

## 6. When do you switch into Aggressive communication styles in your day to day life?

* I speak angrily when I feel stressed or overwhelmed.

* I get aggressive when someone keeps interrupting me.

* I react strongly when I feel disrespected.

* I lose patience and get angry when things do not go as planned.

* I may raise my voice or speak harshly when I am frustrated.

## 7. When do you switch into Passive Aggressive (sarcasm/gossiping/taunts/silent treatment and others) communication styles in your day to day life?

* I stay quiet when I am upset and do not want to say it directly.

* I avoid talking to someone when I think they are being unfair.

* I use sarcasm, small jokes, or silent treatment instead of speaking openly.

* I sometimes talk behind someone’s back instead of being honest.

* I show my anger or frustration indirectly instead of saying it clearly.

## 8. How can you make your communication assertive? You can watch and analyse the videos, then think what would be a few steps you can apply in your own life?

* Speak clearly and politely.

* Use “I” statements like “I feel…” or “I think…” instead of blaming.

* Listen carefully and understand others before speaking.

* Be confident by keeping eye contact and speaking in a steady voice.
